## A BETTER YOU

**Your first and most important assignment is to develop, immediately implement, improve, adjust and re-implement your lifeplan to continuously work on building a more effective YOU. This assignment will consume you for the the rest of your life ... if you try to hide from this assignment, your life will diminish into oblivion.**

There's no alternative. Of course, you will need to have a sense of humor about it, but it has to be the most serious thing in your life.

There are [all kinds of different metaphors that we could use for thinking about this assignment](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/What-are-the-f2J_ponhT8Kt7lhygae3CQ#0) ... and if the top ten metaphors are insufficient to inspire you, you can ask your own AI assistant different levels of follow-up questions ... the point is that the inspiration for doing any of this is entirely up to you ... you have to self-start on this; it's necessarily [***autodidactic***](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/Please-furnish-me-evOl5q9UQraPJ8h5csCtbg) -- you going to need build up your own level of inspiration because the rest of your life is going to be about this lifeplan.

You might choose a flywhell as the best metaphor for spinning up your own effectiveness energy to power through the changing of habits to improve your life, for yourself, for potential employers, for others who matter to you. You start off with what you have and you continually work on accelerating the rotational energy of the flywheel ... if that metaphor doesn't work, maybe you can see yourself winning battles in war ... maybe you metaphor is about constructing a perfect house or climbing the highest peaks in the world ... the important thing is that the metaphor has to work for you, for your personality ... you are going to have to come up with an ongoing cyclic process that works for you continually build up yourself, including during those times when you just don't feel the enthusiasm. 

The process implementing a self-improvement lifeplan is entirely cyclic, like the Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle played out a billion times over, because you will keep circling back to check-adjust-improve. The ORDER of execution might not matter, but the important thing is to hit all of the points everyday and to execute as well as you can, knowing that no day will will be perfect. You will be get better at executing your plan as you continuously keep doing all of the following ten things ... each day.

1) Know your WHY, continuously work at deepening your spiritual life ... when you fail to deepen your spiritual life, the rootedness of your entire existence become shallower and shallower ...  better knowing that WHY and strengthening the health of your soul is the glue that connects your overall implementation of the whole cycle with your VISION every day ... as you move forward, working on your self-improvement plan will become more prayerful, more meditative, more peaceful.

2) Even though you might still be struggling with *the whole vision thing*, you ***have to*** START with gratitude, especially for the limited time you have left on Earth. Never, ever, ever look back longing for the past; don't look in the rear view mirror because you are not going that way. Never wish for the return of old times or expect 2nd chances. Ditch the regrets for what you did not get right. Move forward. Polish the good, REMOVE the bad, eliminate the waste and unnecessary transport, taper off and finally quit the bad habits and addictions that are holding you back ... use D.O.W.N.T.I.M.E. to see and eliminate waste ... don't produce DEFECTS, don't overproduce or overpolish, eliminate activities that predispose anyone to just waiting, NEVER fail to LISTEN -- NEVER waste ideas/interest, eliminate transport -- replace your vehicle with exercise for your legs, reduce inventories to zero, stop moving or pretending you are busy for the sake of looking busy, eliminate ego and refuse to contribute to already overinflated egos ... remember D.O.W.N.T.I.M.E. mnemonic in order to eliminate waste and practice gratitude for the things that matter most

3) Develop a concrete architectural framework of 250 structural traits for practically realizing your vision ... yes, it really is AT LEAST as comprehensive as 250 trait. We'll explore this in greater detail below, but the important thing is that your life needs to have about 250 [or more] different facets **to WORK ON**, ie *it's not about pointing to what you once did* ... anyone who's worked in automotive engineering can tell you why the written specifications for a single automobile model will fill very large rooms of printed requirements and drawing of components, but the features that make your vehicle safe, reliable and useful to you are totally insignificant in comparison with the features/facets of your life. Your framework of traits will a host of things that are infinitely deep such as "better cognitive strategies" "resilience" "leadership" or "communication" ... the important thing is that it's up to you to make these nebulous concepts into concrete, genuine, authentic, USEFUL measurable attributes, eg "In quantitative terms, how good of a listener are you now vs last year; how did you measure that and know that your measurements were somewhat accurate? Precisely, what objective measurements or measurement techniques can you point to, so that you can confidently measure your progress [or lack, thereof]? EXACTLY, what kinds of daily drills and self-assessments do you need to work on to be a better listener?". Your concrete architectural framework of 250 structural traits is the basis of the system that that you will build and deploy in order to build your plan to build a more effective you.

4) You cannot work on 250 facets at once; you will need to prioritize the development of all traits / budget limited time / lay the foundation for plans by setting the scope and priority for action ... as we all know, there are specific things that we MUST accomplish each day; there are things that probably should try to accomplish each day and there are things that we should should opportunistically try to accomplish if happen to be in a certain location or some other unplanned event makes it easier ... AND [if we have eliminated our time-wasting addictions] there will time available when we can tackle lower-priority nice-to-get-done tasks ... your prioritization framework or time management discipline is what will guide you to using your time effectively ... if you cannot use your time effectively, then you will need to work by the hour, per task or on salary per management direction for someone else.  YOU NOT ONLY HAVE TO DEVELOP AN EFFECTIVE TIME MGMT OR PRIORITIZATION FRAMEWORK ... but you also have to develop the competencies for making that framework better.

5) Develop proficiency in setting written Specific Measurable Aggressive Realistic Time-Bound (SMART) goals as you scope your projects to build competency. Written goals are necessary to really drive action plans -- it might be best if your writing on this topic is on paper, so that you can sketch ideas and doodle and emphasize points as you journal your thoughts in the early morning.

6) [Reach out to others](https://www.perplexity.ai/search/How-can-I-TozfCNYxQqWYZyYhUTdOUQ#0); unless you in some sort of locked-down detention ward in a mental institution or prison, you should not let your geography constrain you. Network like a networking fiend as you work on your projects and work through your written goals and action plans. Exploit the tiniest of opportunities to reach out specifically to others doing things that match your passion, match your agenda -- you can be assured that [although they might be too busy to respond to you] they will be extremely glad to hear from you as long as you take the time to effectively communicate your request in a manner that is respectful of their time. You can safely avoid all teevee watchers, passive news consumers, movie buffs, celebrity followers, sports spectators, alcohol/drug users and all others who are not self-starters ... in order to develop networks with people who share your passions, you will need to let the dead bury their dead. 

7) Develop your communciation skillset in order to be able to make new friends serendipitously, build connections and develop new friendships as you fine-tune your plans, pitch ideas or ask questions, propose fixes to issues, empathize with others working on tough issues, develop ways to get people to give you feedback that you might not want to hear

8) How is what you are doing monetizable or valuable enough that others would actually freely contribute to. Continually think in terms of your value proposition to discriminating customers RATHER than thinking merely in terms of being liked. There's not really any value in just being liked. Instead, think in terms of exactly WHO the paying customers is going to be for the "product" that you will be peddling ... you might not have definite individuals identified at first, but even then you will need to think about the target customer, the kind of person who has the wherewithall to become a customer ... as you prospect, potential *customers* for what you are selling, you will immediately need to be qualifying leads as you go in order to spend enough time on those who will financially sustain your endeavors, ie EXACTLY why should a discriminating customer pay for your product?

9) Sell your "product," get used to rejection to the point that you crave rejection, listen to the reasons for the rejection as you tailor your strategy, close the small deals at first -- then close the larger ones, deliver the goods, follow-up and ensure satisfaction, if you are doing tough things, you need to expect to hear complaints and some those rebukes [from paying customers or people you respect] will sting because you will realize that the complaint has merit -- most will try to ignore that, but you need to USE that opportunity. learn from your mistakes, use your recovery from misunderstandings, mistakes and missteps to really drive the referral process from those who know you really care

10) Refuse to ever take any credit; humbly LISTEN, optimistically, cheerfully, enthusiastically give all glory to God as you continuously circle back to the beginning.


## KNOWING YOUR WHY BEGINS WITH YOU DEFINING YOUR SPIRITUAL LIFE.

What is your WHY? If you want to develop into an authentic ORIGINAL, you are going to have to ask yourself some deep questions about what the Origin of your existence really is.

This is not what somebody else tells you ... sure, we all have our influences, but YOUR Why can only come from you listening to you ... from you finding your Creator, your God, your ORIGIN of all origins.

What is your PURPOSE in life? What is your VISION for that purpose? If you are not any good at the *vision thing*, that's a sign that you are adrift as a soulless lost ship at sea ... YOU are going to have become good at the vision thing for your life.

Your mission might be something like working diligently becoming a better Christian, in order to be a unique, different and better example to others ... but YOU still have to define what exactly what that is ... of course, you will have your influences, but you must choose those influences carefully ...  but if you want to develop into an authentic ORIGINAL, you are going to have to ask yourself some deep questions about what the Origin is.

**YOU MUST STOP BEING DEFINED BY THE BOXES THAT OTHERS WANT TO PUT YOU IN.** 

You have to define yourself ... you can fake it a bit, you can start by copying the next guy, except you have to realize that the guy next to you is likely to be the dumbest guy in the room -- his answers might not be wrong for him, but they are almost definitely wrong for you.

For example, you might feel that you are really called to BE the CHURCH, but not to go to church, because you have a circle of friends/family members across the globe that count on you for different things ... so your life/business has always been primarily virtual and/or intercontinental, so your connections to the world almost entirely virtual, thus your church is going to be almost entirely virtual ... you can have a local presense, but your life is where your passions and callings take you ... Jesus Christ was not a bricks and mortar pastor, he was not defined by his geography; Jesus Christ's ministry was as virtual as a ministry could be 2000 years ago.
