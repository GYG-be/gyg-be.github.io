## J. Covenant Relationships

"Covenant" is another one of those loaded words, but we owe it to ourselves to think about exactly what this word means, why it was chosen and exactly how this agreement drives our service. Cultivating Christ-centered relationships marked by mutual respect, trust, and a shared lasting, ideally permanent commitment to biblical values. This involves prioritizing quality time with loved ones, practicing active listening and empathy, and offering forgiveness and grace in times of conflict. It also means surrounding oneself with godly influences and seeking accountability from fellow believers. This intentional approach to relationships fosters an environment of love, support, and spiritual growth.

# 100 Questions for Contemplation

### Understanding Covenant Conceptually

1. **How does my understanding of "covenant" differ from mere contracts or agreements?**

- [Jeremiah 31:31-33](https://www.bibleref.com/Jeremiah/31/Jeremiah-31-31.html) - "The days are coming," declares the LORD, "when I will make a new covenant with the people of Israel and with the people of Judah... - [Hosea 2:19-20](https://www.bibleref.com/Hosea/2/Hosea-2-19.html) - "I will betroth you to me forever; I will betroth you in righteousness and justice, in love and compassion. God uses marriage covenant language to describe His relationship with His people, showing that covenants are deeply relational bonds with emotional and spiritual dimensions that mere contracts lack.

2. **What biblical covenants most inform my understanding of relationship commitments?**

- [Genesis 15:9-18](https://www.bibleref.com/Genesis/15/Genesis-15-9.html) - God makes a covenant with Abraham using ancient covenant-making rituals where animals were cut in half and contracting parties walked between the pieces, signifying "may this be done to me if I break this covenant." - [Ephesians 5:25-27](https://www.bibleref.com/Ephesians/5/Ephesians-5-25.html) - "Husbands, love your wives, just as Christ loved the church and gave himself up for her..."
