**It's important to recognize that since you are TRANSFORMING yourself by removing the bad habits of being yourself, you will be offending those who INSIST on seeing you as a collection of your old bad habits.**  For example, if you have given up alcohol, you cannot hang out with people whose social existences are largely based upon the *enjoyment of* alcohol.  You do not need to be mean about it, but if you are serious about transforming yourself, you will have to leave those people addicted to bad habits that you no longer engage in behind.

Transforming yourself is about being FULLY present ... and clearly communicating that you have chosen to live on the future-looking side of NOW.

Transformation comes down to a very simple choice of REFUSING to live in the past ... it's fine to have pleasant memories of the past; it's NOT OK to waste even part of a second wanting to re-create the past ... the memories that you have of the past are sufficient; you will fail miserably when you insist upon re-creating the past. 

Transformation is very simply about deciding that transformation WILL occur ... in order for this to happen, you WILL have to believe that the future WILL be better ... you will have to believe that the Creator WILL provide a better overall creation.

The transformation that the Creator wants us to make will not be easy or painless.  Without any doubt, pain will be associated with the transformation, but we must believe that such pain really is just weakness leaving the body.  The transformation to a better future will not be easy in a LIVING creation ... but when we LOVE our Creator, we must know that the transformation must happen ... *that most certainly must include whatever might come after this life*.

### Be MORE present, elevate your discipline from where you are

You can improve the culture by being present, but you can also make things worse by choosing to live in the past. 

### For today or right now, focus on NOW and elevating from where you are

People tend to take *where they are, right now* for granted ... to fail to respect all of the pain and sacrifice required to reach where we are.

The point is not to dwell on the past, but RATHER to understand that our future is attainable only by building TODAY upon a very ordinary, routine, nothing special, dull state that we are tempted take for granted. 

### Tomorrow your goals mayb move up based on you completing tasks of elevation

*It'll get HARDER ... one way or another.*

### Time goes one way, you can't live by looking in the rearview mirror 

Focus your mind on planning on tomorrow rather than regretting that it's never be as good as yesterday. The key to ensuring tomorrow gets better is realizing that **YESTERDAY SUCKED** and **anyone who tells you that yesterday was somehow better is delusional, at best ... and basically a detrimental zombie to have around.** You simply cannot tolerate having people around who prefer to live in the past.

# The tough part is leaving people living in the past behind

*Letting the dead bury their dead* is not easy ... it's hard to see people willfully killing their futures. Although there are lessons from History, we are not going to get anywhere by reliving some highlight reel or some trauma that happened two or three or ten or twenty-five or fifty years ago. **Let the dead bury the dead.**

# Embrace the daily grind to overcome complacency 

Git-ing Your GYG is about using your GYG as your GYM **every day**.

In order to find emotional health, strength and courage, work out ... GYG your own GYM ... in a *physical, mental* and especially *SPIRITUAL* sense. When you let yourself get complacent, you basically advance the progression toward death or to that point where life is no longer worth living.

The ONLY way to avoid emotional complacency or to workout that courage *muscle* is to constantly be sharpening the axe and then using that axe see if it's still sharp and to *test if our shoulders, arms and torso can still safely swing a seriously mean axe.* Sharpening and swinging the axe is a metaphor for all kinds of things, but the most important part of the metaphor is **embracing the daily grind** of working out that courage muscle.

Courage does not happen without exercise -- the best way to exercise it is to Git yourself up on the *stage* that is your way to to perform. 

Stop being overcome by the boredom and tedium of your existence.  *How Do We Overcome Tedium?* That eventually can become a VERY SERIOUS question of all of us ... we might even say that it is a *DEAD* serious question for many people after retirement. Many people either tip over and die OR succumb to being couch potatoes or AARP tourists after retirement. 

*For some of us, there might not be ANY question that is more important than "HOW Do We Overcome Tedium?"*

**Tedium kills more people than stress. Atrophy kills more people than working too hard.** 

You can EMBRACE the daily grind. Or die.

## Discipline equals FREEDOM

Git Your Own Gyg is another spin on the "Practice Like You Want To Play" coaching mantra ... just because you are not in a big game today, you still have to practice [and extreme own discipline] like the way that you will want to play in that big game.

When we own our own GYG, **we have to set our agenda.** When we don't set our agenda, something else will ... when we're addicted to material SHIT or some other kind of fritterware like sports or political news, we might as well be a junkie or crack addict.

## Sharpen the axe by always improving the axe sharpening discipline

Of course, you should have some daily routine to prioritize and plan each day in order to optimize each of the other time blocks ... but you really also need to devote a small amount of effort, energy and resources [as you work through your day] to continually improving this daily routine and especially to keep it from becoming a rut.  

If your routine generally working for you, you should not really need or want to tinker with what is working, if you are sure it is genuinely working. 

But if you have "hit a dry spell" or somehow sense that you've gotten bit stale, you will want to improve your daily routine ... or maybe improve part of it, like let's say that your selling and business development skills are not bring in new scores of big game revenue ... but obviously, revenue only takes one so far -- we know that the really BIG GAME is the spiritual game ... maybe we can agree that we all have some part of the AXE that we can sharpen ... and it's best to sharpen that axe before it's seriously dull and someone else has to grind us a new axe.

**If you are working for yourself, you NEED to double down on the commitment to working harder ON yourself.** How can we increase our desire to continually elevate our discipline?  How can we better desire to continually become more creative, to become a better servant of our Creator? Where is our focus or have we lost our focus? When we're addicted to cheap material SHIT or someother kind of worldly fritterware, we might as well be a junkie or crack addict.

When you are legitimately working for your Creator, you cannot EVER be troubled by what others think ... that's why you need to double down on the commitment to work harder on the discipline of being a better version of yourself ... DEVELOPING yourself is what Git Your GYG is about.
