# Discipline equals FREEDOM

Git Your Own Gyg is another spin on the "Practice Like You Want To Play" coaching mantra ... just because you are not in a big game today, you still have to practice [and extreme own discipline] like the way that you will want to play in that big game.

When we own our own GYG, **we have to set our agenda.** When we don't set our agenda, something else will ... when we're addicted to material SHIT or some other kind of fritterware like sports or political news, we might as well be a junkie or crack addict. 

*As human animals, we are neurologically hardwired to seek the comfort of pleasure of things that reliably provide a neurological or mental boost.* 

You cannot overcome your fundamental nature ... but you can ***use*** it. USING Your fundamental neurological nature is the driving forces behind why your Big Why has to be like a crack addiction that won't let go. Your Creator made you this way for a reason ... USE that reason. ***EMBRACE the fact that the Universe was created just for you.*** *OWN IT!*

Your daily discipline has to become like something that you'd crawl through shards of broken glass to get ... something that once the addiction is set results in a drive that deepens with each fix ... this is NOT a rational thing -- your cognitive powers and reasoning ability will play the role of The Serpent telling you that you're better/smarter than that, that you don't have to work on your discipline ... but that does not change the fact that when you are working for yourself, you will need to work harder ON yourself and on the DISCIPLINE of becoming the YOU that you need to be. Nobody else can do it for you. 

It's up to you to constantly work at sharpening your focus and eliminating lazy or unproductive habits.

## Sharpen the axe by always improving the axe sharpening discipline

Of course, you should have some daily routine to prioritize and plan each day in order to optimize each of the other time blocks ... but you really also need to devote a small amount of effort, energy and resources [as you work through your day] to continually improving this daily routine and especially to keep it from becoming a rut.  

If your routine generally working for you, you should not really need or want to tinker with what is working, if you are sure it is genuinely working. 

But if you have "hit a dry spell" or somehow sense that you've gotten bit stale, you will want to improve your daily routine ... or maybe improve part of it, like let's say that your selling and business development skills are not bring in new scores of big game revenue ... but obviously, revenue only takes one so far -- we know that the really BIG GAME is the spiritual game ... maybe we can agree that we all have some part of the AXE that we can sharpen ... and it's best to sharpen that axe before it's seriously dull and someone else has to grind us a new axe.

**If you are working for yourself, you NEED to double down on the commitment to working harder ON yourself.** How can we increase our desire to continually elevate our discipline?  How can we better desire to continually become more creative, to become a better servant of our Creator? Where is our focus or have we lost our focus? When we're addicted to cheap material SHIT or someother kind of worldly fritterware, we might as well be a junkie or crack addict. 

It's important to avoid getting a new job simply to stay busy OR to stay relevant in some narrow professional field ... there are far more important, far bigger things to worry about than just staying busy because that's easier to justify or rationalize to others. 

When you are legitimately working for your Creator, you cannot EVER be troubled by what others think ... that's why you need to double down on the commitment to work harder on the discipline of being a better version of yourself ... DEVELOPING yourself is what Git Your GYG is about.

# Optimize means embrace the daily grind of constantly sharpening

How Do We Overcome Tedium?  How do we avoid complacency?

It's a VERY SERIOUS question ... we might even say that it is a *deadly* serious question. There *might* not be ANY question that is more important.

Tedium drives an exquisitely subtle behavior, something that many can/do hide, something that has been around for a very long time ... but maybe only recently picked up a popular name ... that something is the ol' * *quiet quit*. It's fine to *quiet quit* on a job or some project ... maybe that thing was just sort of undeserving ... but we should never ***openly quit*** or even *quiet quit* on our own WHY.

People are actually plent SMART already about these things; they know ALL about good intentions ... just like other good, gung ho souls, they are more than familiar with the endless array of [different mnemonic devices for goalsetting](https://www.developgoodhabits.com/mnemonic-devices/) ... now that there's an [AI goalsetting utility like Bard](https://g.co/bard/share/13a474d297c8) we even have a bit of journalling robot help us remember some of the why, some of what went into goalsetting ... because we call back yesterday's question and Bard's response -- then we can revise our question in light of our performance in the last 24 hours or we can maybe revise Bard's response and we can integrate Bard into our PDCA cycle of sharpening and sharpening and sharpening on the BIG WHY. 

Overcoming tedium is about incorporating new tricks, new technologies, new ways to keep coming at the same old problem from a new angle ... **DISCIPLINE EQUALS FREEDOM** is all about grinding against the tedium and USING THE TEDIUM to sharpen, renew, revitalize our focus on serving our Creator ... to look further ahead, to map out new goals and waypoints on the drive toward that BIG WHY.
