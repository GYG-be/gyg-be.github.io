## MVP Viable PROCESS Minimums ... ONE of them is the MVP, minimally viable product

PROCESSES go into building a startup ... so the GYG.be StartupCookbook cover the Minimally Viable PROCESSES that either necessary or highly recommended PROCESS to be used in building a startup ... the Minimal Viable PRODUCT is just ONE of several meta-tasks or PROCESSES that a startup must do. 

Obviously, developing your venture around an MVP PRODUCT is ONE of several of the processes that the GYG.be startup cookbook STRONGLY URGES new startups to START WITH ... without a minimal viable PRODUCT, there's not really a need to be out there marketing anything and try to get early adopters to consider ... and without some likely sales that come after qualified leads have had a chance to test something like a proof-of-concept prototype in their business ... there's not going to much need legal organizational framework or anything like a financial management to pursue financing ... all of these frameworks will start of being kind of sketchy at first, but they will rapidly come together when you have a serious MVP product that people want to buy.

For us at GYG.be, it's ALL about building an open source clonable habitat for startup ecoystems ... but the point of a startup ecosystem for nanoenterprise ideas is get people off on the right foot and not fail learning some lesson that someone else has already learned ... it's like a COOKBOOK for people STARTING TO LEARN to cook. There are some common themes that we see in the realm of building SUCCESSFUL startups ... there are things that WORK and things that DON'T WORK ... if you follow a recipe in a cookbook scrupulously, there's still no gaurantee that your dinner will be a success ... but we at least want to be aware of the way the recipes say we should do it in order to give success a chance to happen ... *this doesn't rule out improvisation but, improvisation can increase the odds of a very painful failure.*

# BEGINNING By Focusing On Exit To FUNDED Unicorn Status

There might be exceptions, but as a general rule GYGsters, or people that follow the general GYG philosophy, do not want to adopt the GYG philosophy with the intention of settling for being employees or for working on other people's projects.

Git-ting Your GYG is about building something that kind of represents who you are or what you aspire to be ... and, in the case of VENTURES for enteprises that employ people and serve customers ... well, ultimately, since none of us lives forever, the ventures that we build will need to be passed on to others and maybe someday acquired by larger entities.

A new GYG venture is intended to becoming a sustainable venture with a million-dollar valuation that will attract extra interest and financing to be acquired by a larger entity or ideally something that will attract funding to become a billion-dollar UNICORN.

## Focusing on Unicorn Commonalities and Best Practices

The recipes for approximately what we want our VENTURES [that GYGsters build] is something that we will sketch out with StartupCookbook by studying best practices of the [1366 currently active billion-dollar startup unicorns](https://pitchbook.com/news/articles/unicorn-startups-list-trends) ... as well as also by learning from the mistakes of others that failed along the way in the quest to become a unicorn ... but the most important thing is to focus on plugging to what really works in the startup ecosystem and the venture capital ecosystem ... the aim is to develop ventures that have the kinds of exit strategies that are most likely to lead to a successful handoff of a venture.

3) Quantinuum is a developer of quantum computing applications designed for the development of applications across chemistry, cybersecurity, finance, and optimization. 10) RefleXion is a developer of a biology-guided radiotherapy designed to create a new cancer treatment modality. The company's radiotherapy leverages positron emission tomography (PET) in a novel way and combines it with stereotactic radiotherapy for superior localization and tumor tracking at the time of treatment delivery, enabling physicians to reveal cancer cell location and then destroy it.

# General process for developing our startup cookbook

As we look at case studies of successful unicorns, we want to generally employ a process that allows us to develop an understanding of how and why the unicorn was successful. We want to understand the unicorn's business model, the unicorn's product, the unicorn's market, the unicorn's team, the unicorn's technology, the unicorn's financing, the unicorn's exit strategy, and the unicorn's competition.

# 80/20 Rule Or Pareto Principle

Perhaps it's not 80/20, maybe more like 95/5 ... but, other than the "bright idea" or company concept, what seems to be the company's most important small percentage cost driver that accounts for the vast majority of the company's success?

# 5S and LEAN D.O.W.N.T.I.M.E.

How does this company eliminate different kinds of waste to make the world simpler, more efficient, more effective, more orderly, more rational?

# TRIZ and inventive problem-solving

What is this company's most inventive approach to problem-solving? What does it do that can be thought of as somewhat similar to TRIZ's Contradiction Matrix or 40 Principles?
